The Grand Final of the Pinke Socke Song Contest 2024 took place in Bosse Theatre, Hohenried, on Saturday 11 May.

Venue & Location
Bosse Theatre, Hohenried
Host Broadcaster

Swedish broadcaster Pinksockiges Television (PST), together with the Pinke Socke Broadcasting Union (PSBU), organises the Contest thanks to Anfeen's historic win in Hohenried in 2023.

It was the third time that Hohenried hosted the Pinke Socke Song Contest.

Sweden itself hosted the Pinke Socke Song Contest for the first time in 2024.

United By Socks

Following its hugely successful use by the PPC, it was agreed by PST and the Pinke Socke Song Contest Reference Group (the Contest governing board), to use United By Socks as the official slogan for the Pinke Socke Song Contest in 2024, as well.

Furthermore, it was decided to keep the slogan for all successive events as part of the Contest's global brand strategy.

The Theme Art

'The Pink Lights' served as the theme art for the 3rd Pinke Socke Song Contest.

'The Pink Lights' is a concept based on simple linear gradients, inspired by the vertical lines that permeate both the northern lights and sound equalizers, and was created to be a versatile identity that can be adapted according to need and devices.

9 Participants

Sock-puppet broadcasters from 9 countries took part in the Pinke Socke Song Contest when the world’s biggest live music event was staged in Hohenried in May 2024.

The Running Order of the Grand Final was revealed the day before the Live Show, in the early hours of Friday 10 May.

The Hosts

The 3rd Pinke Socke Song Contest was hosted by a Hollywood star and an iconic Pinke Socke veteran; Teddy Åkerman and Falkie Mede together led the broadcast, live from Bosse Theatre.

The Grand Final

9 songs competed in the Grand Final on Saturday 11 May. All 9 countries voted, along with a Rest of the World vote.

Switzerland topped the Grand Final vote with 591 points, thanks to The Code performed by XYZ. It was Switzerland's first Pinke Socke Song Contest win.

Croatia finished in second place with 547 points, after winning the televote. Ukraine, France and Israel completed the Top 5 of the scoreboard.
